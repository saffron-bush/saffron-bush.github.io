I once had a friend say to her mum "I don't need to keep going to school, I will just use street smarts like Saffron" I was honored but also a little perplexed, what did she mean by street smarts? and if I was street smart, what was book smarts?

Book smarts or what you would call IQ, stands for Intelligent quotient and is a measure of a person's reasoning ability. It is used to gauge how well someone can use information and logic to answer questions or make predictions. IQ focusses on:

  • Measuring long and short term memory
  • Knowledge of the world
  • Fluid reasoning
  • Quantitative reasoning
  • Visual and spatial processing

So if that is IQ (book smarts), then what is street smarts? Well there is a different part of your brain that processes a different type of intelligence. That intelligence is called Emotional Intelligence, EQ can be defined as how well we perceive, evaluate and express emotions both with ourselves and in our relationships with others. People with high EQ know what they're feeling, what their emotions mean and how those emotions can affect other people. To cultivate this you need to do: Attention Training - this is where you are training our body to become more aware of the mind to cultivate a clear, calm and alert mind. Practicing Self-Knowledge and Self Mastery - by becoming more attentive to yourself and creating useful mental habits - develop habits to defend against our critical mind when it comes up.

There are 5 parts to EQ:

  1. Self awareness- knowing what we are feeling and why we are feeling that way. By tapping into this it creates good intuition and decision making and a good moral compass.

    How to develop this skill? Keep a journal. Slow down when you experience strong emotions to examine yourself in that moment without self criticism

  2. Self regulation - handling your distressing emotions in effective ways.

    How to develop this skill? Tune into these emotions when needed. Know your values. Hold yourself accountable (admit your mistakes). Practice being calm

  3. Motivation - Mastering positive emotions - Aligning actions with passion.

    How to develop this skill? Have high standards for the quality of your work. Re-examine why you are doing your job/study (set goals), know where you stand - if you are feeling unmotivated own it and work on it. Be hopeful and find something good - try to find at least one good thing about the situation

  4. Empathy - knowing what someone else is feeling.

    How to develop this skill? Put yourself in someone else's position. Pay attention to body language. Respond to feelings (when you hear disappointment notice that and respond). Use active listening.

  5. Skilled relationship - Be open to hearing both good and bad news. Become a master in giving and receiving support and getting buy-in from others By doing this, you become good at managing change and resolving conflict diplomatically.

    How to develop this skill? Learn conflict resolution. Improve your communication skills. Learn how to praise others.

Neuroplasticity - shapes brains long term. So learning the above will shape your brain in a positive way. Not only do you benefit by listenintg to youself but it also helps you to become more in tune with others. Research shows when doing this: Antisocial behavior goes down Prosocial behavior goes up Academic skills go up Fact: Interestingly, as environments have become more information rich and sophisticated over the past 100 years, IQ results have dramatically been going up.
