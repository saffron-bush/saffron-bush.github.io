I Don't Need To Keep Going To High School, I Will Just Use Street Smarts

Emotional Intelligence and it's effect on my learning journey

14 February 2022

♥ Happy Galentines day peeps ♥

Speaking of gal friends, I once had a friend say to her mum "I don't need to keep going to high school, I will just use street smarts like Saffron" I was honored but also a little perplexed. What did she mean by street smarts? and if I was street smart, what was book smarts?

Book smarts or what you would call IQ, stands for Intelligent quotient and is a measure of a person's reasoning ability. It is used to gauge how well someone can use information and logic to answer questions or make predictions. IQ focusses on:

  • Measuring long and short term memory
  • Knowledge of the world
  • Fluid reasoning
  • Quantitative reasoning
  • Visual and spatial processing

So if that is IQ (book smarts), then what is street smarts? That intelligence is called Emotional Intelligence, EQ can be defined as how well we perceive, evaluate and express emotions both with ourselves and in our relationships with others. People with high EQ know what they're feeling, what their emotions mean and how those emotions can affect other people. EQ is a learned skill just like everything else and to cultivate this you need to: train your body to become more aware of the mind to cultivate a clear, calm and alert mind (aka Attention Training). Become more attentive to yourself (aka Practicing Self-Knowledge and Self-Mastery) and develop habits to defend against our critical mind when it arises (aka Creating Useful Mental Habits).

So had I mastered those skills when my friend mentioned street smarts?...I doubt it. Daniel Goldman talks about 5 parts to EQ and how you can start mastering these skills:

  1. Self awareness- knowing what we are feeling and why we are feeling that way. By tapping into this it creates good intuition and decision making and a good moral compass. How to develop this skill? Keep a journal. Slow down when you experience strong emotions to examine yourself in that moment without self criticism
  2. Self regulation - handling your distressing emotions in effective ways. How to develop this skill? Tune into these emotions when needed. Know your values. Hold yourself accountable (admit your mistakes). Practice being calm
  3. Motivation - Mastering positive emotions - Aligning actions with passion. How to develop this skill? Have high standards for the quality of your work. Re-examine why you are doing your job/study (set goals), know where you stand - if you are feeling unmotivated own it and work on it. Be hopeful and find something good - try to find at least one good thing about the situation
  4. Empathy - knowing what someone else is feeling.How to develop this skill? Put yourself in someone else's position. Pay attention to body language. Respond to feelings (when you hear disappointment notice that and respond). Use active listening.
  5. Skilled relationship - Be open to hearing both good and bad news. Become a master in giving and receiving support and getting buy-in from others By doing this, you become good at managing change and resolving conflict diplomatically.How to develop this skill? Learn conflict resolution. Improve your communication skills. Learn how to praise others.

Neuroplasticity - is the belief that your brain is always growing and changing with each experience you have. Repeated tasks then solidifies this as a behaviour and shapes our brains long term. In other words, the more we practice the above, the easier it will be ingrained into our everyday behaviour. Shaping your brain in a positive way to become in tune with yourself and others.

Research shows that by doing this:

  • Antisocial behavior goes down
  • Prosocial behavior goes up
  • Academic skills and performance go up
  • Those in leadership positions perform much higher
  • and there is an increased sense of Happiness

so was my friend right? Should we all just quit school and just become street smart? Nah, there is still a place for book smarts (especially wth learning to code) So as I increase my IQ through this journey, I will also be working on my EQ because... Everything is a Learnable Skill.
